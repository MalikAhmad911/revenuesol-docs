Email campaigns let you send branded, targeted messages to your contacts straight from Revenue Sol. Use them for seasonal promotions, maintenance reminders, post-job follow-ups, newsletters, and re-engagement of dormant customers. Because your campaigns draw from the same CRM that powers your inbox, jobs, and reputation tools, every send stays in sync with the contact data you already trust.

## Build your audience

Start by deciding who should receive the message. Revenue Sol pulls your audience directly from your CRM, so you can target the right customers without exporting spreadsheets.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a starting list">
    Send to all contacts, or narrow to a saved segment such as customers in a specific service area, lead stage, or job type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter with tags and segments">
    Layer on [tags and segments](/crm/contacts) to refine recipients, for example "HVAC maintenance plan" or "leads with no activity in 90 days."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm contactability">
    Only contacts with a valid email address and who have not unsubscribed are included. Revenue Sol handles this filtering for you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Smaller, well-targeted audiences almost always outperform a blast to everyone. A message about furnace tune-ups means more to homeowners who bought a heating system from you than to your whole list.
</Tip>

## Create the content

Compose your email using a template or build from scratch. You can personalize messages with merge fields like the contact's first name so each email feels one-to-one.

- **Subject line.** Keep it short and specific. This is the single biggest driver of whether your email gets opened.
- **Body.** Add your logo, images, headings, and buttons. Link to a booking page, a [quote](/quotes/estimates), or a [payment link](/payments/payment-links) to turn the email into action.
- **Call to action.** Make the next step obvious, such as "Book your spring service" or "Reply to schedule."

<Info>
Unsubscribe handling is built in. Recipients can opt out, and Revenue Sol automatically suppresses them from future sends to keep you compliant and protect your sender reputation.
</Info>

## Send or schedule

Preview your email and send a test to yourself before going live. When you are ready, send immediately or schedule the campaign for a specific date and time, for example a Tuesday morning when inboxes are most active.

<Warning>
Double-check your audience size and subject line before sending. Once a campaign is sent, it cannot be unsent. Always send a test email to yourself first.
</Warning>

## A/B testing

To improve results over time, set up an A/B test. Create two versions, most commonly two subject lines, and Revenue Sol sends each variant to a portion of your audience so you can see which performs better.

## Review your metrics

After a campaign sends, open its report to see how it performed. Track core metrics including delivered, open rate, click rate, unsubscribes, and bounces. Use these numbers to refine future subject lines, content, and timing.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why is my open rate low?">
    Open rate is mostly driven by the subject line, sender name, and send time. Test shorter, more specific subject lines and avoid spammy words.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What causes bounces?">
    Bounces happen when an email address is invalid or rejects the message. Keeping your contact data clean reduces bounces and protects deliverability.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I automate emails instead of sending one-off campaigns?">
    Yes. Use the [automations](/marketing/automations) engine to trigger emails from events like a completed job or a new lead.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
