Connecting RingCentral lets Revenue Sol place and receive calls and texts through your own RingCentral number. Calls and texts are billed by RingCentral at carrier cost with no markup, so you keep the number and rate plan you already have while Revenue Sol handles the conversation, AI receptionist, and unified inbox on top.

<Info>
RingCentral is one of two phone providers you can bring to Revenue Sol. If you use Twilio instead, see [Twilio](/integrations/twilio). You only need one provider connected.
</Info>

## Connect your RingCentral account

You authorize Revenue Sol from the integrations area of your workspace. Connecting uses RingCentral's secure sign-in, so you never share your password with Revenue Sol.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the RingCentral integration">
    In your workspace settings, find the integrations or phone connection area and choose RingCentral.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize access">
    You're redirected to RingCentral to sign in and approve access. Approve the requested permissions so Revenue Sol can send and receive calls and SMS on your behalf.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select an SMS-capable number">
    After authorizing, pick the RingCentral number you want Revenue Sol to use. Choose a number that has SMS enabled on your RingCentral plan so texts route correctly into your inbox.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the connection">
    Once the number is selected, Revenue Sol shows the connection as active. Send a test text or place a test call to confirm messages and calls land in your [unified inbox](/inbox/overview).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Choosing a number that is not SMS-capable means calls work but texts won't deliver. If you're unsure, check your RingCentral admin portal or enable messaging on the line in RingCentral before selecting it here.
</Warning>

## Keep the connection healthy

RingCentral access is granted through a token that can expire or be revoked, especially if your RingCentral password changes, an admin removes the app's access, or the account's security settings are updated. When that happens, calls and texts may stop flowing into Revenue Sol.

<Tip>
Watch your inbox for a banner or status indicator that RingCentral needs attention. If you see one, reconnect promptly so your AI receptionist and messaging keep running without gaps.
</Tip>

To reconnect, return to the RingCentral integration and authorize again. Re-authorizing refreshes the token and restores call and SMS routing without changing your number or losing your conversation history.

## Import past messages

When you connect, you can bring recent RingCentral message history into Revenue Sol so your team sees the full context of each customer conversation rather than starting from an empty thread. Imported messages are matched to your [contacts](/crm/contacts) where possible and appear alongside new activity in the unified inbox.

<Note>
Importing brings in your existing SMS history; it does not change anything inside RingCentral. The import runs after you finish connecting and may take a little time depending on how much history you have.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Texts aren't being delivered">
    Confirm the number you selected is SMS-capable in RingCentral. If messaging isn't enabled on that line, calls will still work but texts will fail.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Calls and texts suddenly stopped">
    The most common cause is an expired or revoked token. Reopen the RingCentral integration and re-authorize to restore the connection.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I changed my RingCentral password">
    Password and security changes can invalidate the connection. Reconnect Revenue Sol to RingCentral to issue a fresh token.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
