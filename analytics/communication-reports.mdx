Call and message reports show you how much your business communicates and how fast you respond. In home services, speed wins jobs: the contractor who answers first usually books the work. These reports turn your calls, texts, emails, and web-form leads into numbers you can act on, so you can spot slow responses, missed calls, and after-hours gaps before they cost you revenue.

## What these reports cover

You get a clear view of communication activity across every channel in your [unified inbox](/inbox/overview):

- **Call volume** — total inbound and outbound calls, including missed and answered calls, over the period you choose.
- **Message volume** — SMS, email, and web-form conversations, broken down by direction.
- **Response times** — how long it takes for a lead to get a first reply, measured from when their message or call arrives.
- **AI vs. human handling** — how much was handled automatically by your [AI receptionist](/ai/receptionist) and [AI Employee](/ai/ai-employee) versus picked up by your team.

<Note>
Calls and texts run on your own Twilio or RingCentral number, so volume here matches your carrier activity. Revenue Sol adds no markup on carrier usage.
</Note>

## Reading your numbers

Open Analytics and select the communication reports. Use the date range and channel filters to focus on what matters.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set your time window">
    Pick a range that matches how you work — a week to catch trends, a month for a fuller picture.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compare AI and human handling">
    See what share of conversations the AI answered on its own and which ones it routed to your team. A healthy mix means the AI is catching after-hours and overflow contacts you would otherwise miss.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check response time">
    Find your average and slowest first-response times. Slow replies are the clearest signal of lost jobs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Spot missed calls and gaps">
    Look for missed calls and unanswered messages clustered at certain hours or days, then close those gaps.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Improving speed-to-lead

Speed-to-lead is how fast a new lead gets a meaningful response. Faster responses book more jobs. Use these reports to drive it down:

<Tip>
A reply within the first few minutes dramatically improves your chance of booking. Treat any first-response time longer than five minutes as a fix-it metric.
</Tip>

- **Let AI cover the gaps.** If reports show missed calls or slow replies after hours or during busy field days, lean on the AI receptionist and AI Employee to answer instantly, every time. The AI draws on your [Knowledge Hub](/ai/knowledge-hub) to reply accurately.
- **Automate the first touch.** Set up an [automation](/marketing/automations) that texts every new web-form or missed-call lead immediately, so no one waits.
- **Watch the trend, not just the day.** Track first-response time week over week to confirm changes are sticking.
- **Route faster.** If certain channels lag, adjust who gets notified on the [mobile app](/mobile/overview) so the right person responds first.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How is response time measured?">
    It is the time from when an inbound call or message arrives to the first reply, whether that reply comes from the AI or a team member.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do AI-handled conversations count toward my volume?">
    Yes. Every call and message is counted, and reports separate what the AI handled from what your team handled.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Where do web-form leads appear?">
    Web-form leads land in your unified inbox alongside calls, texts, and email, and are included in message volume and response-time reporting.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
