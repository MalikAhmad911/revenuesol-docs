Automation recipes are pre-built workflows you can copy into your account and customize. Each recipe pairs a trigger (something that happens in Revenue Sol) with one or more actions (a text, an email, a task, or a stage change) so routine follow-up happens without anyone lifting a finger. Use these as starting points, then tweak the timing and wording to match your business.

<Note>
Recipes live in the automations engine. Open a recipe, adjust the message text and delays, connect your sending channel (your Twilio or RingCentral number for SMS), and turn it on. You can edit any step after activating.
</Note>

## Missed-call text-back

Never lose a lead because no one could pick up. When a call goes unanswered, this recipe texts the caller within seconds.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set the trigger">Choose the missed or abandoned call trigger so the recipe fires the moment a call ends without being answered.</Step>
  <Step title="Send an instant text">Reply from your business number with a short message like "Sorry we missed you! How can we help?" so the lead can text back.</Step>
  <Step title="Route the reply">New replies land in your [unified inbox](/inbox/overview), where the AI Employee or a teammate can continue the conversation.</Step>
</Steps>

## Estimate follow-up

Quotes that sit unopened rarely close. This recipe nudges customers who have not approved an estimate.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trigger on a sent estimate">Start the workflow when a [quote or estimate](/payments/quotes) is sent but not yet approved.</Step>
  <Step title="Wait, then follow up">After 2 days with no response, send a friendly text or email reminding them the estimate is ready to review and e-sign.</Step>
  <Step title="Add a second touch">If still unapproved after 5 days, send one more nudge and create a task so a rep can call.</Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Include the approval link in the message so customers can e-sign in one tap from their phone.
</Tip>

## Review request after a job

Turn finished work into Google reviews automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trigger on job completion">Fire the recipe when a [job](/jobs/overview) is marked complete.</Step>
  <Step title="Wait a short window">Pause a few hours so the customer has had time to enjoy the result.</Step>
  <Step title="Send the review request">Text a personalized Google review link. New reviews can get AI-written replies from the [reputation](/reputation/overview) tools.</Step>
</Steps>

## No-show reminder

Cut down on missed appointments with timely reminders before a scheduled visit.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trigger on a scheduled job">Start when a job or appointment is booked on the calendar.</Step>
  <Step title="Remind the day before">Send an SMS confirming the date, time, and arrival window the evening before.</Step>
  <Step title="Send a morning-of text">Send a final reminder a couple of hours ahead with an easy way to reschedule.</Step>
</Steps>

## Reactivation of old customers

Win back past customers who have gone quiet.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build the audience">Use [tags or segments](/crm/contacts) to find customers with no job in the last 6 to 12 months.</Step>
  <Step title="Send an offer">Text or email a seasonal tune-up or a return-customer discount.</Step>
  <Step title="Follow up once">If there is no reply after a week, send a single gentle reminder, then stop.</Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Keep automated SMS frequency reasonable and always include opt-out handling. Carrier costs for calls and texts pass through at cost on your own Twilio or RingCentral number.
</Warning>
