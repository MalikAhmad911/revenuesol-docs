Your AI Employee and 24/7 AI receptionist are only as good as what you teach them. A few habits keep replies accurate, on-brand, and trustworthy enough to run around the clock. Use the tips below to dial in your setup, then run the checklist before you let the AI handle live conversations on its own.

## Keep the Knowledge Hub fresh

The [Knowledge Hub](/ai/knowledge-hub) is the source of truth your AI Employee draws from. Outdated content is the most common cause of wrong answers.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sync your website and FAQs">
    Train the Knowledge Hub on your current website and FAQ content so the AI quotes real services, policies, and details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update after every change">
    Whenever you change prices, add a service, or shift your service area, refresh the Knowledge Hub the same day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove stale entries">
    Delete seasonal promos and discontinued services so the AI never offers something you no longer provide.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Add the questions customers actually ask on calls and texts. Real phrasing trains the AI to recognize and answer real intent.
</Tip>

## Set clear hours and services

Ambiguity forces the AI to guess. Spell out exactly what you do and when.

- Define your business hours and after-hours behavior so the AI knows when to book versus when to take a message.
- List your services and service area precisely so the AI does not promise work you do not cover.
- State what requires a human, such as complex quotes or emergencies, so the AI hands off instead of improvising.

## Review approvals early

When you first turn on the AI Employee, treat it like a new team member. Review its drafted replies in your [unified inbox](/inbox/overview) before they send.

<Note>
  Approval-first mode lets you catch tone and accuracy problems before a customer ever sees them. Once you trust the results in a given scenario, you can let those replies send automatically.
</Note>

Watch for replies that overpromise, miss context from earlier in the thread, or sound off-brand. Each correction teaches you what to add to the Knowledge Hub.

## Monitor transcripts

Read call transcripts and message threads regularly, especially in the first few weeks.

- Confirm the [AI receptionist](/ai/receptionist) booked jobs correctly and captured the right details.
- Look for repeated questions the AI could not answer, then add those answers to the Knowledge Hub.
- Flag any conversation where the AI should have handed off to you and adjust your handoff rules.

## Tune the tone

Your AI should sound like your business, not a generic bot.

- Set the voice and personality to match how your team actually talks to customers.
- Keep replies concise and warm; long, formal answers feel robotic in SMS.
- Add brand phrases and a consistent sign-off so every channel feels like the same company.

<Warning>
  AI replies consume AI credits, which are included on every plan. If responses stop sending, check your remaining credits in [billing](/account/billing).
</Warning>

## Quick checklist

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Before you go live">
    - Knowledge Hub trained on current website and FAQs
    - Business hours and after-hours behavior set
    - Services and service area listed accurately
    - Tone and sign-off configured to match your brand
    - Handoff rules defined for emergencies and complex requests
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Weekly">
    - Review a sample of transcripts and drafted replies
    - Add answers for any questions the AI missed
    - Refresh the Knowledge Hub after price or service changes
    - Confirm AI credits are sufficient for your volume
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
