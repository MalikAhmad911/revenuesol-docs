Revenue Sol lets you bring your own Twilio phone number to power calls and text messages across the platform. When Twilio is connected, your number drives the 24/7 AI receptionist, the unified inbox, SMS campaigns, automations, and text-to-pay. Because it is your own account, calls and texts are billed by Twilio at carrier cost with no markup from Revenue Sol.

## What you need

Before you connect, gather these details from your Twilio account:

- Your **Account SID** (your Twilio account identifier).
- Your **Auth Token** (used to authorize Revenue Sol to send and receive on your behalf).
- A **phone number** purchased in Twilio that is both voice- and SMS-capable.

<Info>
Because you bring your own Twilio account, calls and texts are billed directly by Twilio at standard carrier rates. Revenue Sol does not add a markup on call or message usage.
</Info>

## Connect Twilio

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the integrations settings">
    In Revenue Sol, go to your integrations settings and choose Twilio as your phone and SMS provider.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your credentials">
    Paste your Account SID and Auth Token from the Twilio Console. Revenue Sol uses these to send and receive calls and messages through your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your number">
    Choose the Twilio number you want Revenue Sol to use. This becomes the number your customers call and text, and the one your outbound messages come from.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and verify">
    Save the connection. Revenue Sol confirms the credentials are valid and configures your number to route incoming calls and texts into the platform.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
If you do not have a Twilio number yet, purchase a voice- and SMS-capable local number in the Twilio Console before you connect. For a guided walkthrough of choosing and connecting your business number, see [/getting-started/connect-phone-number](/getting-started/connect-phone-number).
</Tip>

## What connecting enables

Once your Twilio number is live, it powers the communication features across Revenue Sol:

- **AI receptionist** answers calls and texts around the clock and books jobs.
- **Unified inbox** brings every call and SMS into one conversation view alongside email and web-form leads.
- **SMS campaigns and automations** send texts from your number.
- **Text-to-pay and payment links** can be delivered by SMS so customers pay from their phone.

## Compliance and deliverability

US carriers require application-to-person (A2P) registration for SMS. Make sure your Twilio number and messaging are properly registered in Twilio so your texts deliver reliably. Keep your Auth Token private; treat it like a password and rotate it in Twilio if you suspect it has been exposed.

<Warning>
If you regenerate your Auth Token in Twilio, your existing connection stops working until you update the credentials in Revenue Sol.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My number connected but texts are not sending">
    Confirm your number is SMS-capable and that your A2P messaging registration is complete in Twilio. Unregistered or pending numbers are often blocked by US carriers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Calls are not coming into the inbox">
    Re-save the Twilio connection so Revenue Sol can reconfigure your number's routing, and verify the number you selected is voice-capable.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Authorization errors after connecting">
    Your Account SID or Auth Token may be incorrect or recently rotated. Copy fresh values from the Twilio Console and reconnect.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
Prefer not to use Twilio? Revenue Sol also supports RingCentral as a bring-your-own-number provider. See [/integrations/ringcentral](/integrations/ringcentral).
</Note>
