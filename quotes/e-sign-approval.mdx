When you send a quote from Revenue Sol, your customer doesn't have to print, scan, or call you back. They open a secure link, review the details, and approve and e-sign the quote right from their phone or computer. This page walks you through what your customer sees, how you're notified, and how to turn an approved quote into a job and invoice.

## How customers review and approve a quote

After you send a quote, the customer receives it by text or email with a link to a hosted, mobile-friendly view. No login or app download is required.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the quote">
    The customer taps the link in the SMS or email. The quote opens in their browser, showing your business name, line items, quantities, pricing, and any totals or taxes you've included.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the details">
    They can scroll through each line item and any notes or terms you've added so they understand exactly what's included before committing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve and e-sign">
    The customer accepts the quote and adds their electronic signature to confirm. The signed approval is recorded with a timestamp on the quote.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The approval link is tied to that specific quote. If you edit and resend a quote, the customer reviews the updated version before signing.
</Note>

## Notifications when a quote is approved

The moment a customer approves and signs, the quote status updates to approved in Revenue Sol so you and your team can see it across the CRM.

You also get notified through the channels you've set up, including push notifications on the [mobile app](/mobile/overview) so field and office staff know in real time. The signed approval and timestamp stay attached to the quote as your record of the customer's consent.

<Tip>
Use the [automations engine](/marketing/automations) to trigger follow-up actions on approval, such as creating a task to schedule the work or sending the customer a thank-you message.
</Tip>

## Converting an approved quote into a job

Once a quote is approved, the next step is usually getting the work on the calendar. From the approved quote you can convert it into a [job](/jobs/overview), carrying the line items and customer details over so you don't re-enter anything.

From there you can schedule the job, assign it on the [dispatch board](/jobs/dispatch), and send appointment reminders. Because the contact and quote stay linked, you keep a clear history from first lead through completed work.

## Converting an approved quote into an invoice

When the work is done (or when you collect a deposit up front), turn the approved quote into an [invoice](/payments/invoices). The approved pricing flows into the invoice automatically.

You can then send the invoice with text-to-pay or a payment link so the customer pays by card through Stripe. For ongoing work, you can set up recurring payments. See [payments](/payments/overview) for the full range of options.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do customers need an account to sign?">
    No. The quote opens in a browser from the link you send. There's no login, app, or account required.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I require a signature, or is approval enough?">
    Quotes capture both the customer's approval and their electronic signature, giving you a signed, timestamped record of their acceptance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if the customer wants changes?">
    Edit the quote and resend it. The customer reviews the updated version and signs that one. Reach out to <a href="mailto:contact@infiniterankers.io">contact@infiniterankers.io</a> if you need a hand.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
