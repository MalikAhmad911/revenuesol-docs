Revenue Sol connects to the phone systems, payment processors, accounting tools, and lead sources your home-service business already relies on. Connecting an integration unlocks features across the platform, from the AI receptionist answering calls to invoices syncing into your books, so you manage everything from one place instead of juggling tabs.

You set up integrations once in your workspace settings. Most use a guided connect flow that signs you in to the partner service and authorizes Revenue Sol to act on your behalf.

<Note>
AI features are included on every plan, but capabilities like voice AI and review replies depend on the relevant integration being connected. Start with your phone provider and Stripe to get the most value quickly.
</Note>

## What each integration unlocks

- **Twilio and RingCentral** provide the phone number that drives inbound and outbound calls, SMS, the [AI receptionist](/ai/receptionist), and the [unified inbox](/inbox/overview). You bring your own number, and carrier costs pass through at no markup.
- **Stripe** handles money: card payments, [text-to-pay](/payments/text-to-pay), [payment links](/payments/payment-links), recurring billing, and refunds tied to your [invoices](/payments/invoices) and quotes.
- **QuickBooks** keeps your accounting in sync by mirroring contacts, invoices, and payments.
- **Google** runs your [reputation](/reputation/overview) workflows (automatic review requests and AI-written replies) and feeds [web analytics](/analytics/web-analytics) from Search Console and GA4.
- **Retell** is the voice AI that lets the receptionist hold natural conversations and book work around the clock.
- **Meta** captures leads and messages from Facebook and Instagram so no inquiry slips through.
- **Webhooks and Zapier** extend Revenue Sol to custom and third-party tools when there isn't a native connection.

## Connect an integration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Integrations settings">
    Go to your workspace settings and find the Integrations area, where each available connection is listed with its current status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the connect flow">
    Select the integration you want and follow the guided steps to sign in to the partner service and authorize access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and configure">
    Once connected, choose any options the integration offers, such as selecting the phone number to use or which Google location to manage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify it's working">
    Send a test message, place a test call, or check that data appears where you expect it before relying on the connection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You can connect both a phone provider (Twilio or RingCentral) and Stripe during [onboarding](/getting-started/onboarding) to get calls, texts, and payments live on day one.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

  <Accordion title="Do I have to pay extra for calls and texts?">
    No. You bring your own Twilio or RingCentral number, and carrier usage is billed at cost with no markup from Revenue Sol.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use more than one integration at the same time?">
    Yes. Most businesses run a phone provider, Stripe, Google, and often QuickBooks together. Each integration works independently.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if the tool I use isn't listed?">
