title: "Connect your phone number"
description: "Bring your own Twilio or RingCentral number so calls and texts run through Revenue Sol."
---

Revenue Sol uses a bring-your-own-number model. You connect your own Twilio or RingCentral phone number, and from then on calls, texts, and the 24/7 AI receptionist all run through Revenue Sol while billing for actual call and message usage stays directly with your carrier.

## Why bring your own number

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pay carrier cost, no markup">
    Calls and texts are billed by Twilio or RingCentral at their standard rates. Revenue Sol does not add a per-minute or per-message markup on top.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keep your existing number">
    Your business number stays yours. Customers keep calling and texting the number they already know, and your account is fully portable.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Because you own the carrier account, you stay in control of your number, your usage, and your spend. Revenue Sol handles the software layer: answering, routing, logging conversations into your unified inbox, and powering the AI receptionist.

<Info>
  You need an active Twilio or RingCentral account before connecting. AI features and the 14-day free trial are included on every Revenue Sol plan, but live calls and texts require a connected number.
</Info>

## Choose Twilio or RingCentral

Both providers work with Revenue Sol. Pick the one that fits your business:

- **Twilio** is a good fit if you want a flexible, developer-friendly carrier and are buying a new number or porting one in. It is often the simplest path for businesses that do not already have a phone system.
- **RingCentral** is a good fit if you already run a RingCentral phone system and want to keep your existing setup while adding Revenue Sol's AI and CRM features.

## How to connect

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open phone settings in Revenue Sol">
    Sign in at [app.revenuesol.com](https://app.revenuesol.com) and go to your phone or communication settings, where you can connect a provider.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your provider">
    Select Twilio or RingCentral as the carrier you want to connect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize the connection">
    For RingCentral, you authorize Revenue Sol through RingCentral's secure sign-in. For Twilio, you provide your account credentials so Revenue Sol can send and receive on your number. Follow the integration page for the exact fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your number">
    Pick the phone number you want to use for calls and texts. This becomes the number your AI receptionist answers and the number that appears on outbound messages.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a test">
    Place a test call or text to confirm everything routes into your unified inbox correctly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  After connecting, configure your [AI receptionist](/ai/receptionist) and [Knowledge Hub](/ai/knowledge-hub) so calls and texts are answered accurately around the clock.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My calls or texts are not coming through">
    Confirm the number is selected in Revenue Sol and that your Twilio or RingCentral account is active and funded. Then send another test from an outside phone.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will this change how customers reach me?">
    No. Customers keep using your existing business number. Revenue Sol simply answers and routes the conversation.
  </Accordion>
