Revenue Sol lets you bring your whole team into one workspace and give each person exactly the access they need. Whether you're a solo operator with a single dispatcher or a growing shop with office staff and field techs, roles keep your data secure and your workflows tidy.

## Invite teammates

You manage everyone who can sign in from your account settings, in the **Team** (or **Members**) area.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your team settings">
    Sign in at [app.revenuesol.com](https://app.revenuesol.com) and go to your workspace settings, then open the Team section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send an invite">
    Add the teammate's email address and choose the role you want to assign. They'll receive an email invitation to join your workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="They accept and set a password">
    Once your teammate accepts, they create their own login and appear in your member list as active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Invite people with their work email so notifications, call routing, and activity history stay tied to the right person.
</Tip>

## Roles and permissions

Roles determine what each teammate can see and do. Higher-access roles can manage billing, integrations, and other users, while limited roles focus on day-to-day work like the unified inbox, jobs, and the [dispatch board](/jobs/dispatch).

| Capability | Owner / Admin | Standard member | Limited / Field |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Manage billing & plan | Yes | No | No |
| Add or remove teammates | Yes | No | No |
| Manage integrations | Yes | Limited | No |
| Work the inbox, leads, and jobs | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| View reports & analytics | Yes | Yes | Limited |

<Note>
The exact role names and permission breakdown can vary by plan. Open the role selector when inviting a teammate to see the current options for your account.
</Note>

Assign the most restrictive role that still lets someone do their job. Field techs typically only need access to their assigned jobs and customer details, while office staff handle the inbox, quotes, and invoicing.

</Info>

## Remove a user

When someone leaves your business or changes roles, remove or deactivate their access right away to protect your customer data.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the teammate">
    Go to the Team section and locate the person in your member list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove or deactivate them">
    Use the remove option next to their name. Their login is revoked and their seat is freed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reassign their work">
    Reassign any open conversations, leads, or jobs that belonged to them so nothing slips through the cracks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Removing a user revokes their access immediately. Historical activity (calls, messages, and notes they created) stays in your records for continuity.
</Warning>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I change someone's role later?">
    Yes. Open the teammate in your Team settings and update their role at any time. Changes take effect the next time they refresh the app.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to leads assigned to a removed user?">
    Their assignments stay in place until you reassign them. Move open work to an active teammate to avoid gaps in follow-up.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I add more seats?">
    Upgrade your plan from your billing settings. If you need a custom seat count, contact us about Enterprise.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
