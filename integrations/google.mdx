Connecting Google gives Revenue Sol access to your **Search Console** and **Analytics (GA4)** data, so you can track how customers find you and how your website performs — right alongside your leads and revenue.

## What connecting Google enables

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Search Console" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Track keyword rankings, impressions, and clicks.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Analytics (GA4)" icon="chart-line">
    See website traffic and engagement.
  </Card>
  <Card title="URL indexing" icon="link">
    Request indexing of important pages in Search Console.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Property selection" icon="list-check">
    Choose which sites and analytics streams to track.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Before you start

<Note>
You'll need a **Google account** with access to a Search Console property and/or a GA4 property. Revenue Sol connects via Google's secure OAuth.
</Note>

## Connect Google

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Integrations → Google">
    Find the Google integration (also reachable from the Analytics area).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Connect Google">
    You'll be sent to Google's consent screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant access">
    Approve read access to Search Console and Analytics.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select properties">
    Choose which Search Console properties and GA4 streams Revenue Sol should track.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Data syncs">
    Your rankings and analytics populate in the dashboard. You can trigger a manual sync anytime.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Analytics in Revenue Sol

Once connected, your Search Console and GA4 data appear in the Analytics dashboards, so you can see search performance and site engagement next to your CRM activity. You can also export analytics reports to PDF.

<Tip>
Connect Google early so you build a history of search and traffic data — trends are far more useful than a single snapshot.
</Tip>
