Connecting QuickBooks Online keeps your books in sync with Revenue Sol — invoices, payments, and customers flow into QuickBooks automatically, so you don't double-enter data.

## What syncing enables

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Invoice sync" icon="file-invoice">
    Invoices created in Revenue Sol appear in QuickBooks.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Payment sync" icon="money-bill-transfer">
    Payments reconcile against the right invoices.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Customer sync" icon="user">
    Customers stay consistent across both systems.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Reconciliation" icon="scale-balanced">
    A scheduled job keeps transactions reconciled.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Before you start

<Note>
You'll need a **QuickBooks Online** account. Revenue Sol connects via Intuit's secure OAuth — you'll sign in to QuickBooks and authorize access to your company (realm).
</Note>

## Connect QuickBooks

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Integrations → QuickBooks">
    In Revenue Sol, find the QuickBooks integration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Connect QuickBooks">
    You'll be redirected to Intuit to log in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize access">
    Grant Revenue Sol access to your QuickBooks company's accounting data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sync runs">
    Syncing begins automatically. You can also trigger a manual **Sync now** anytime.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Keeping data in sync

After connecting, Revenue Sol pushes new invoices and payments to QuickBooks and runs a scheduled reconciliation to keep transactions aligned. You can run a manual sync whenever you want to push changes immediately.

<Tip>
If you're testing, connect a QuickBooks **sandbox** company first to verify the sync before pointing it at your live books.
</Tip>
