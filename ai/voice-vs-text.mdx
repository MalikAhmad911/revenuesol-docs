Revenue Sol gives you two distinct AI surfaces that cover every way a customer might reach your home-service business. The **voice receptionist** answers your phone, and the **text AI Employee** handles SMS, web-form leads, and email. They look like one assistant to your customers because they share the same brain, but each is tuned for a different channel.

## The voice receptionist

The voice receptionist is a 24/7 AI that picks up calls on your business number when you can't. It greets callers, answers common questions, captures contact details, and books jobs straight onto your calendar, day or night.

Use the voice receptionist when:

- A caller dials your number after hours, during a job, or while every line is busy.
- You want missed calls turned into booked appointments instead of lost leads.
- A customer prefers to talk rather than type.

Calls route through your own Twilio or RingCentral number, so carrier minutes are billed at cost with no markup. Voice handling is powered by Retell behind the scenes.

## The text AI Employee

The text AI Employee works across your written channels: inbound SMS, web-form submissions, and email. It auto-replies in your brand voice, qualifies leads, answers questions, and can move a conversation toward a booking, all from the unified inbox where your team also works.

Use the text AI Employee when:

- A lead texts your number or fills out a form on your website.
- You want instant first responses so prospects don't go cold.
- A conversation is better handled in writing, with links, quotes, or confirmations.

The text AI Employee runs on **AI credits**, which are included on every plan.

## What they share

Both surfaces draw from the same **Knowledge Hub**, which you train on your website content and FAQs. That means a caller and a texter get the same accurate answers about your services, hours, pricing, and service area, no matter which channel they choose.

<Note>
Update your Knowledge Hub once and both the voice receptionist and the text AI Employee stay in sync. See [Knowledge Hub](/ai/knowledge-hub) for how to add and refine sources.
</Note>

Everything either AI does also flows into the same CRM and [unified inbox](/inbox/overview): contacts are created or matched, leads land in your pipeline, and bookings appear on your dispatch calendar.

## When each one is used

<Steps>
  <Step title="A customer calls">
    The voice receptionist answers, helps the caller, and books or logs the lead.
  </Step>
  <Step title="A customer texts, emails, or submits a form">
    The text AI Employee replies and continues the conversation in the inbox.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Either way, the work shows up in one place">
    The interaction is saved to the contact, the lead pipeline, and your calendar so your team has full context.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You don't have to choose one or the other. Turn both on so no lead is missed regardless of how it arrives.
</Tip>
