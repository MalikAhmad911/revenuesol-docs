Online booking lets your customers schedule jobs themselves, around the clock, without a phone call. You share a booking link, the customer picks a service and an open time, and the appointment lands directly on your calendar. Combined with the 24/7 AI receptionist, it means your business can capture and book work even while your crews are in the field or off the clock.

## How online booking works

You publish a self-service booking page tied to your account. When a customer opens the link, they choose the service they need, see the time slots you have available, and confirm their details. The request becomes a booked job in Revenue Sol, the customer gets a confirmation, and reminders go out automatically before the appointment.

<Note>
Online booking respects the availability you define, so customers can only choose times you actually want to take work. Slots that are full or outside your hours never appear.
</Note>

## Share your booking link

Once your services and availability are set, you get a shareable booking link you can put anywhere customers find you.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open online booking settings">
    Go to the Jobs area and open the online booking settings to find your booking link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the link">
    Copy the link and add it to your website, Google Business Profile, email signature, SMS replies, and social profiles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test it yourself">
    Open the link in a private browser window and walk through a booking to confirm the services and times look right.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Add the booking link to the auto-reply messages your AI Employee sends, so leads who text in can book without waiting for a callback.
</Tip>

## Set your services and availability

Customers book against the services and hours you configure, so it's worth getting these right before you share the link widely.

- **Services**: Choose which offerings from your catalog customers can book online, along with how long each one takes. Pull from your existing [products and pricing](/payments/products-and-pricing) so durations and naming stay consistent.
- **Availability**: Define the days and hours you accept online bookings, plus any buffer time between jobs and how far in advance customers must book. This prevents back-to-back appointments with no travel time.
- **Capacity**: Limit how many bookings can land in the same window so you don't overbook your crews.

<Warning>
If you leave availability wide open, customers may book times you can't realistically staff. Set your real working hours and buffers so the calendar reflects what your team can actually deliver.
</Warning>

## How booked slots flow into your calendar and AI receptionist

Every online booking is treated as a real appointment, not a separate to-do list:

- The booking appears on your [calendar](/jobs/calendar) and is available to assign on the dispatch board.
- The customer record is created or matched in your [contacts](/crm/contacts), keeping all their history in one place.
- Automatic reminders go out before the appointment to cut down no-shows.
- Because availability is shared, the AI receptionist sees the same open slots. When it answers a call or text, it books into the same calendar, so phone bookings and self-service bookings never collide or double-book.

<Info>
Whether a job comes in by phone with the AI receptionist or through your booking link, it lands on one unified schedule. You manage everything from a single calendar.
</Info>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Customers say no times are available">
    Check that your availability covers upcoming days and that your booking lead time and capacity limits aren't blocking every slot.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="A service isn't showing up">
    Confirm the service is enabled for online booking and has a duration set in your catalog.
