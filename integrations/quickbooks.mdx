Connect Revenue Sol to QuickBooks Online so the invoices and payments you create in the field flow straight into your accounting, without double entry. Once linked, your bookkeeper sees the same numbers you do, and you keep selling instead of re-typing transactions.

## What syncs

The QuickBooks Online integration keeps your billing records aligned across both systems. When you connect, the following data flows from Revenue Sol into QuickBooks:

- **Customers** — Contacts you bill are matched to QuickBooks customers, or created if they do not exist yet.
- **Invoices** — Invoices you send from Revenue Sol appear in QuickBooks with their line items, amounts, and status.
- **Payments** — When a customer pays, including card payments and text-to-pay collected through Stripe, the payment is recorded against the matching QuickBooks invoice.

<Note>
QuickBooks syncs your billing and accounting records. It does not move your jobs, quotes, or CRM activity. For how invoices are created and sent, see [Invoices](/payments/invoices).
</Note>

## Connect QuickBooks Online

You connect QuickBooks through a secure authorization flow, so you never share your accounting login with Revenue Sol.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the QuickBooks integration">
    Go to the Integrations area in your Revenue Sol settings and find the QuickBooks card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in to QuickBooks">
    Choose to connect, then sign in to your QuickBooks Online account and authorize Revenue Sol. Use an account with admin access to the company you want to sync.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the company">
    Select the QuickBooks company you want to link. If you manage more than one company, double-check you picked the right one before continuing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the connection">
    Return to Revenue Sol and confirm the QuickBooks card shows as connected. New invoices and payments will sync from this point forward.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Connect only one QuickBooks company per Revenue Sol account. Linking the wrong company can post invoices and payments to the wrong books.
</Warning>

## Keeping your books in sync

After the initial connection, the goal is for both systems to always agree. A few habits keep things clean:

- **Match customers carefully.** Use consistent names and contact details so each Revenue Sol contact maps to a single QuickBooks customer instead of creating duplicates.
- **Let payments record against the right invoice.** Collecting payment on the invoice in Revenue Sol, rather than recording it separately, keeps the linked QuickBooks invoice marked paid.
- **Reconcile periodically.** Review your QuickBooks register against your Revenue Sol invoices to catch mismatches early, especially after refunds or edits.

<Tip>
Decide where you make edits and stick to it. Creating and sending invoices from Revenue Sol, then treating QuickBooks as your accounting system of record, avoids conflicting changes on the same transaction.
</Tip>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Will my old invoices sync retroactively?">
    The integration is designed to keep new activity in sync from the time you connect. Historical records already in QuickBooks should be reconciled manually rather than re-imported.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to refunds?">
    Refunds you issue in Revenue Sol affect the related payment. Always reconcile refunds in QuickBooks so your books reflect the corrected balance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I disconnect QuickBooks?">
    Yes. Disconnecting from the QuickBooks card stops future syncing. Records already synced remain in QuickBooks.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
